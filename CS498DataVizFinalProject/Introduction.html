Malaria and its Impact on the World Population

Malaria is one of the most common infectious diseases in the world today. It is a life threatening disease, caused by parasites that are transmitted to humans, through the bites of infected female Anopheles Mosquitoes. In 2015, according to World Health Organization data, this disease was endemic in 91 countries, affected over 200 million people, and was responsible for over 400,000 deaths. The Visualization contained on this webpage attempts to show how this disease has affected the human population, what regions and countries it is most prevalent in, and what parts of the population are most affected by it.

All the Data presented in this Visualization was sourced from the World Health Organization Website . Facts about the disease, and its impact on the World Population were obtained from the 2016 Annual Malaria Report published by the WHO.

Several elements of this Interactive Slide show Visualization were inspired by examples of d3 visualizations created by Mike Bostock, the founder of the d3.js library, on his excellent Website All the said examples on this website were distributed under the GNU General Public License.

The implementation stack for all the pages in this Visualization is Bootstrap, HTML, CSS, Javascript and D3

About the Visualization

This Visualization follows the Interactive Slideshow hybrid structure. Each slide features a visualization that was built using d3.js. These visualizations allow the user to interact with the data, within the confines of each slide, and consequently glean more information from them, before progressing to the next stage of the narrative. Each slide has a section that describes how that specific visualization effectively utilizes Scenes, Annotations, Parameters and Triggers.
