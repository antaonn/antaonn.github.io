Regional Impact (2015)

In 2015, the most cases of Malaria were reported in the WHO Africa Region (92%). This was followed by the WHO South East Asia Region (6%), and the Eastern Mediterranean Region (1.7%). There were a total of 191 Million cases Malaria reported in the Africa region, 14.4 Million cases reported in the South East Asia Region, 3.8 Million Cases reported in the Eastern Mediterranean Region, 1.2 Million cases reported in the Western Pacific Region, and 0.8 Million Cases reported in the Americas region. No cases were reported in the European region.

In 2015, 90% of the Deaths caused by Malaria occurred in Africa, 7% of the Deaths caused by Malaria occurred in South East Asia, and 2% of the Deaths caused by Malaria occurred in the Eastern Mediterranean Region. Malaria was responsible for a total of 394,000 deaths in Africa, 26200 deaths in South East Asia, 7300 deaths in the Eastern Mediterranean Region, 1500 Deaths in the Western Pacific Region, and 490 deaths in the Americas.

Use the User Input buttons below to view the 2015 Malaria Cases by Region, or the 2015 Malaria Deaths by Region, in the Visualization.

About the Visualization

This Visualization follows the Interactive Slide show hybrid structure. Slide 2 depicts a Scene that conveys to the user, the impact of Malaria on various regions of the World Population. The World Population is divided into 6 regions, based on the World Health Organization classification of Regions. These regions include Africa, South East Asia, Eastern Mediterranean, Western Pacific, the Americas and Europe. The Visualization in this scene is a circle area chart, where each region is represented by a circle. The area of each circle is proportional to either the number of cases of malaria reported in the Region, or the Number of Deaths caused by Malaria in the Region, depending on the users selection of whether to view a graph of the Cases, or a graph of the Deaths. The Triggers in this Visualization include the User's selection of a button that determines whether they want to view a graph of the Malaria Cases by Region, or a graph of Malaria Deaths by Region. This selection drives a Parameter that transitions between the two datasets, and animates the Visualization to display the graph that the user wishes to see. Additional Triggers in this scene include Mouse Over and Mouse Out events. When a user Mouses over a circle on the graph, it displays a Tool Tip Annotation , that shows the number of reported cases/deaths in a region. It also changes the opacity of the circle that is moused over. The annotations are removed, and the opacity is returned to default, when the user triggers a mouses out event. Each circle in the Graph is also Annotated to show the region that the circle corresponds to, and the number of cases/deaths in Millions/Thousands , based on the user selection of whether to view Cases/Deaths. Additional Parameters in this Interactive Slide show are defined by the Slide Numbers that the User can click on, to advance the presentation. These are provided both in a navigation bar at the top of the page, and as Navigation buttons at the bottom of the page. These Parameters determine which slide is displayed to the user. The user can Trigger a change in the scenes contained within the Slide show, by modifying these Parameters.
