Age Group Impact (2010-2015)

In 2015, Malaria was responsible for 303,000 deaths, in Children Under the Age of 5. This is significantly higher than the number of deaths in the Over 5 age group (126,000). Malaria was responsible for 70% of the Deaths in Chidren Under the Age of 5, World wide.

The number of Deaths caused by Malaria in persons above the age of 5 stayed fairly steady between 2010 and 2015 (On Average 123,500). However the number of Deaths caused by Malaria, in Children Under the Age of 5, decreased by 29% during that time span.

Hover over a bar in the chart, to see the number of Deaths associated with that specific group

About the Visualization

This Visualization follows the Interactive Slide show hybrid structure. Slide 5 depicts a Scene that conveys to the user, the impact of Malaria, in terms of number of deaths caused, in 2 distinct age groups, between 2010 and 2015 . The Age groups depicted in this Visualization are the Above 5 age group and the Under 5 Age group. The Chart in this scene is a stacked bar graph, where the Above 5 age group is shown in the lower set of bars, and the Under 5 age group is shown in the upper set of bars. The Triggers in this Visualization include Mouse Over and Mouse Out Events. Mousing Over a bar, triggers a Parameter that Annotates the Graph with the number of Malaria related deaths, corresponding to that Bar. It also changes the opacity of the bar that is moused over. The annotations are removed, and the opacity is returned to default, when the user triggers a mouses out event. Additional Annotations in this graph include a legend showing which colored bars, correspond to which age group, and the associated axes of the bar graph. Additional Parameters in this Interactive Slide show are defined by the Slide Numbers that the User can click on, to advance the presentation. These are provided both in a navigation bar at the top of the page, and as Navigation buttons at the bottom of the page. These Parameters determine which slide is displayed to the user. The user can Trigger a change in the scenes contained within the Slide show, by modifying these Parameters.
